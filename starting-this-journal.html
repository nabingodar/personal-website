Starting This Journal

← All entries

I've rebuilt this website three or four times now, and every version got stuck at the same place: I kept treating it like a résumé instead of a record. A résumé tells people where you've been. It doesn't tell them what any of it felt like, or what you're still turning over in your head at the end of the day.

So this is the version where I stop doing that.

"Travel changes you. As you move through this life and this world, you leave marks behind, and the world leaves marks on you."

That line has stayed with me since I first read it, and it's close to the reason this journal exists. Nepal, then Japan, and soon the UK — each place has left a mark, and most of those marks never made it into any application form or LinkedIn summary. They lived in conversations, in long walks, in the specific way a place teaches you to notice things.

What you'll find here

Three kinds of entries, roughly. Travel notes — actual places, actual moments, written close to when they happened rather than smoothed over in hindsight. Ideas — the things I can't stop thinking about, from Nepal's trade policy to whether a UAP sighting holds up to scrutiny. And every so often, something in between: a reflection that only makes sense once you know where I was standing when I had it.

I'm writing this from Pokhara, near the jungle at the edge of town, with a stream I've walked beside more times than I can count. It felt like the right place to start.

More soon — from here, and from wherever comes next.
